💡 Camping Tips & Money Savers
Insider advice for campervan travel in Iceland

🃏 Iceland Camping Card

A single card that gives 28 nights of camping for 2 people across 170+ campsites. At roughly €199–249 (≈$215–270), it can save you significant money on campsite fees vs. paying individually (~$45–70/night per site).

  • Best for: Trips 10+ nights staying at member sites
  • Note: Not all sites are members — Hellissandur is smaller and may not participate. Verify at campingcard.is
  • Buy online: campingcard.is — also available at some campsites
🃏 Get Camping Card

⛺ Tjalda.is — Official Booking Platform

Iceland's official campsite booking system. Most registered campsites use it. Always book registered campsites — wild camping in campervans is illegal in Iceland since 2015.

  • Search by region or campsite name
  • Some sites also have direct booking
  • Payment on arrival often accepted, but reservation still required in peak season
  • App available for iOS and Android
📅 Go to Tjalda.is

🚿 Shower Strategy

Iceland's municipal pools (sundlaugar) are everywhere and usually charge $5–10 for entry — this includes hot showers and hot tubs. Even if you don't swim, many travelers buy pool entry just for a hot shower and soak.

  • Grundarfjörður Pool: Small town pool, very local
  • Ísafjörður Pool: Great full facility
  • Laugardalur Pool, Reykjavik: One of the best in Iceland
  • Campsite showers: usually coin-operated (200–500 ISK ≈ $1.50–$3.50)

🛒 Food & Grocery Tips

Eating out in Iceland is expensive. Plan 60–70% meals in the van for the Westfjords especially.

  • Bónus: Cheapest grocery chain (yellow pig logo) — stock up in Reykjavik and Ísafjörður
  • Krónan: Second cheapest
  • Nettó: Also budget-friendly
  • Gas stations (N1, Orkan): Have hot food — surprisingly decent & convenient
  • Westfjords: Very limited grocery options — bring several days of supplies from Ísafjörður

📱 Useful Apps to Download

  • Tjalda: Campsite booking & map
  • Vedur.is: Iceland's official weather app (trust this over Google)
  • 112.is: Iceland's emergency safety app — register your trip
  • Maps.me: Offline maps (essential for Westfjords)
  • Safetravel.is: Road conditions & travel alerts
  • Eclipse Safari: Eclipse path & timing app

🌡️ August Weather & What to Pack

  • Average temp: 10–14°C (50–57°F) — bring layers
  • Rain: Pack waterproof jacket & pants — it will rain
  • Wind: Stronger than expected, especially Westfjords
  • Eclipse glasses: Bring certified ISO 12312-2 glasses — may be hard to find locally
  • Midnight sun: Blackout blinds for the van are essential — sun barely sets in August
  • Hiking boots: Waterproof, broken-in

🐱 Cat Cafés & Unexpected Gems

A few spots that align with your interests that don't need advance booking but are worth seeking out:

  • Kattakaffihúsið (Cat Café, Reykjavik): Iceland's first cat café — cozy, resident cats, great coffee. No booking usually needed.
  • Settlement Centre, Borgarnes: Excellent Viking history exhibit — on your drive south from Westfjords. Budget 2 hours.
  • Icelandic Goat Center, Háafell: Near Húsafell — interactive farm with endangered Icelandic goats. Charming and free.
  • Reykjavik Botanical Garden (Grasagarðurinn): Free, beautiful, adjacent to Laugardalur Campsite — perfect arrival-day garden walk.
  • Wool & Craft shops: Handprjónasambandið (Handknitting Association of Iceland) in Reykjavik for authentic lopapeysa sweaters.
